For corporate marketing managers evaluating their first custom website project, the hardest question to answer is also the most common: "How much will this cost?"

The short answer: custom websites in Malaysia typically range from **MYR 30,000 to MYR 250,000** or more, depending on complexity. That range is so wide it is almost useless without context.

This guide breaks down what influences the cost, what a typical investment looks like by project type, and how to evaluate whether custom development is worth it for your organisation.

## What influences the cost of a custom website?

Five factors determine the investment required for any custom web project:

**1. Project complexity.** A five-page corporate brochure site is fundamentally different from a customer portal with account management, reporting, and third-party integrations. Complexity multiplies every other cost factor.

**2. Design scope.** Custom design starts with user research, wireframing, and multiple rounds of high-fidelity mockups. Projects using pre-built templates skip this entirely. A custom visual identity can account for 20-30% of the total budget.

**3. Content management requirements.** Do your marketing team members need to update the site independently? Then you need a CMS — and depending on the complexity of your content model, that means anything from a simple backend to a fully bespoke editorial interface.

**4. Integrations.** Every integration with an external system (CRM, ERP, payment gateway, marketing automation) requires planning, development, testing, and maintenance. Budget 10-40 hours per integration.

**5. Post-launch support.** A good agency does not disappear after launch. Factor in 30 days of included support, then ongoing monthly maintenance.

## Typical investment ranges by project type

### Corporate websites: MYR 30,000 - MYR 80,000

A custom-designed, professionally developed corporate website with 5-15 unique page designs, content management for the marketing team, contact forms, analytics, and mobile-optimised performance. Suitable for most SMEs and corporate divisions looking to establish a premium digital presence.

### Content-heavy marketing sites: MYR 60,000 - MYR 150,000

Sites with extensive content, multi-language support, blog or news sections, resource libraries, and complex navigation. Usually includes 20-50+ pages, a headless CMS for marketing team autonomy, multi-language support, and advanced SEO setup.

### Web applications: MYR 80,000 - MYR 250,000+

Custom applications like customer portals, booking systems, or internal dashboards. Scope varies enormously — user authentication, database architecture, custom business logic, admin interfaces, third-party integrations, and scalable cloud infrastructure all factor in.

### E-commerce platforms: MYR 60,000 - MYR 200,000

Custom shopping experiences beyond what Shopify or WooCommerce can offer. Custom product catalogs, optimised checkout flows, Malaysian payment gateway integrations (iPay88, Billplz, FPX), inventory management, and order fulfillment workflows.

## Custom vs. template: the real cost comparison

"Why pay MYR 50,000 when I can buy a WordPress template for MYR 2,000?"

It is a fair question with a nuanced answer. Templates work perfectly for simple brochure sites. But for businesses where the website is a critical revenue channel, the true cost of templates is hidden in:

- **Customisation overhead.** Templates are rarely a perfect fit. Customisation often reaches 50-70% of the price of a custom build.
- **Performance limitations.** Templates load slowly and fail Core Web Vitals, hurting SEO and conversions.
- **Maintenance burden.** Plugin conflicts, security patches, and compatibility issues consume hours monthly.
- **Scalability ceiling.** When your needs outgrow the template, you start over from scratch.

For any organisation where the website generates meaningful revenue, custom development typically delivers 3-5x ROI within 12 months compared to template alternatives.

## What you should expect from a transparent agency

A reputable custom development agency will provide a detailed written proposal after discovery, not a vague verbal estimate. They will break down costs by phase, explain what is included and what is not in plain language, offer transparent hourly rates for any work outside the initial scope, and include post-launch support as a standard part of the engagement.

Red flags to watch for: quotes without discovery calls, "fixed price" quotes for ambiguous requirements, no written scope document, no mention of post-launch support, and unclear ownership of the final code.
